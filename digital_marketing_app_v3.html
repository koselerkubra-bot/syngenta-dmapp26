Slides 9–14 · Regulatory Adaptation · January 13, 2026

New Regulation.
New Content Model.

Direct digital promotion of CP products restricted — a constraint turned into competitive advantage

The New Regulation
The new Crop Protection Products Regulation published by the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry of the Republic of Türkiye introduces significant restrictions on the promotion of crop protection products via the internet and social media. The regulation came into effect on January 13, 2026.

"Lead with the Problem,
Not the Product."

We shifted our content strategy from product-first to disease and pest awareness-first.
New Approach — How It Works
1

Every month, map which diseases and pests are entering their peak season

2

Build social media content around those threats — not around products

3

Once engaged, direct users to website to discover CP solutions effective against specific threats

✓

Product discovery driven by need, not promotion — fully compliant while maintaining audience engagement

Strategic Outcome and Evaluation
When the classic product-oriented content shared in 2025 is compared with the new content model implemented after the regulation in 2026; its performance in 2026 has notably strengthened in terms of both reach and user engagement.

As a result, the information and awareness-based content approach applied in 2026 has increased the quality of interaction — the content has not only reached more users but also directed users to participate with brand content and created a more sustainable content structure.
Slides 15–21 · AI-Powered Innovation in Agricultural Education

Object Talk — New in the Game

What brings us 284,000 organic visits with a single post — a potential global sector first

WHY – The Motivation
  • New regulations banned certain CP product promotions — Syngenta Türkiye needed to keep growing its channels
  • Low engagement with traditional disease-related content on social platforms
  • Complex agricultural information needed to be more accessible and memorable for growers
WHAT – Strategy & Execution
  • Concept: AI-powered anthropomorphic storytelling where plants and diseases 'speak' to tell their own stories
  • Format: Short-form vertical video reels featuring talking plants (Mildew, Rust Diseases in Wheat)
  • Tools: ChatGPT (prompts) → Google Whisk (image) → Google Flow (video) → Canva (concept)
  • Delivery: Instagram Reels with both organic and paid promotion strategies
IMPACT – The Results
  • Reach: 284,000+ organic | 45,282 paid reach
  • Engagement: 278% increase vs. total 2026 content | 2,060+ likes, 117 shares, 61 saves
  • Followers: 1,200+ new in one week (5% of total base) from 2nd Object Talk
  • Efficiency: CTR 3.09% | CPC $0.023 | Shares +838% | Saves +274%
  • 🏅 Content appeared in Instagram Explore section — organic reach extended beyond paid

Object Talk Strategic Conclusion & Evaluation
Content published in Object Talk format produced high user interaction in a short time; it has shown a remarkable performance in reach, views and follower acquisition metrics, especially on the Instagram side.
In the comparison, a single Object Talk content generated higher engagement compared to the total performance of the content shared throughout 2026; total engagement increased by 278%, while sharing (838%) and saves (274%) metrics increased significantly.
The second content in the Syngenta Object Talk series gained over 1,200 new followers in one week with organic reach alone. For an account active since 2018 with a total follower base of approximately 24,400, a single piece of content grew the account organically in just one week by approximately 5% of its total follower base. This data reveals that content establishes a strong connection with the target audience, and organic engagement is measurably reflected in follower conversion.
